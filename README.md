# incident-intelligence-decision-support-playbook
Executive decision support playbook demonstrating intelligence-led incident response, cyber risk assessment, and stakeholder communication.

## Executive Summary
This playbook models an intelligence-led approach to managing cyber incidents by integrating adversary analysis, multi-source intelligence, and risk assessment to reduce uncertainty during time-sensitive response scenarios. The project illustrates how cyber threat intelligence informs incident triage, containment decisions, and executive-level risk discussions across security, fraud, and business stakeholders.

## Scenario
A large U.S. enterprise detects indicators of potential credential compromise and early-stage ransomware activity. Initial alerts provide technical signals but limited context. Leadership requires intelligence-driven assessment to determine incident severity, likelihood of escalation, and potential business impact in order to guide response and resource allocation decisions.

## Intelligence Objectives
- Assess adversary tools, techniques, and tradecraft
- Support incident response prioritization and escalation
- Evaluate cyber risk and potential business impact
- Translate technical findings into executive-ready insights

- ## What This Playbook Demonstrates
- Cyber threat intelligence analysis of adversarial behavior
- Intelligence-informed incident response workflows
- Threat-driven cyber risk assessment and mitigation planning
- Multi-source intelligence synthesis (OSINT, dark web, incident artifacts)
- Stakeholder-focused intelligence communication

- ## Repository Structure
- `reports/` – Intelligence products supporting incident response and executive decisions  
- `frameworks/` – Intelligence workflows and stakeholder communication models  
- `lessons-learned.md` – Reflections on intelligence effectiveness and decision impact

- ## Practical Application
In a production environment, this playbook would support security leadership, incident response teams, fraud units, and executives by providing decision-ready intelligence during active cyber incidents. The approach emphasizes clarity, prioritization, and risk-based decision support over raw data delivery.

## Audience
This playbook is intended for cyber threat intelligence analysts, incident response leaders, risk managers, and executive stakeholders responsible for managing cyber risk within complex enterprise environments.
